IUJ's student body is small and international — clubs are the fastest way to build a real social network and find people outside your immediate program cohort.

> 🔶 This article needs club list verification and senior input. The list below is a starting point — update with current active clubs each year.

---

## Why Join a Club

- IUJ is isolated — clubs structure your social life in a way that passively attending class doesn't
- Cross-program mixing: MBA and IR students tend to cluster separately in class; clubs break this
- Resume value: leadership roles in student orgs are legitimate for early-career profiles
- Vacation planning: sports and travel clubs often organize group trips — cheaper and more fun than going alone
- Many clubs are involved in the wiki expansion process (see [[10_Travel & Leisure/Vacation Planning/How to Plan & Budget|Vacation Planning — How to Plan & Budget]])

## Types of Organizations

### Academic & Professional
- **Student Government / Student Council** — represents student interests to IUJ administration; organizes campus events
- **Model UN Club** — particularly relevant for IR students; competition participation possible
- **Entrepreneurship / Business Club** — MBA-heavy; case competitions, guest speakers
- **Research Circles** — informal groups organized around thesis topics or faculty research
- **TEDxIUJ** — student committee that runs an annual TEDx event on campus (independently licensed TEDx event, ~100-seat audience); also sells event merchandise

### Sports & Fitness
- **Football (Soccer) Club**
- **Cricket Club**
- **Basketball Club**
- **Badminton Club**
- **Table Tennis**
- **Yoga / Wellness group**
- ⚠️ Confirm current active sports clubs — these fluctuate with each cohort

### Cultural & Social
- **International Cultural Exchange Club** — organizes cultural nights, food events
- **CAT Program participants** — see [[CAT Program|What is CAT?]]
- **Film Club** (if active)
- **Music / Jam sessions** — informal but consistent at IUJ
- **Language Exchange groups** — pair up with Japanese students for mutual language practice
- **UMEX** — not an IUJ club, but a local Minamiuonuma volunteer/exchange group that regularly invites IUJ students to potluck-style events (e.g. a New Year's party at Cafe No Problem in Urasa) featuring Japanese traditional dance, tea ceremony, and games; a good low-key way to meet locals outside CAT

### Interest-Based
- **Photography Club**
- **IUJ Alpine and Trekking Society** — the hiking club's official name; open to beginners and experienced hikers alike, framed around Japanese mountaineering traditions
- **Cooking Club** — practical and social; relevant for [[Cooking Basics & Cheap Meal Prep]]

## How to Join

- Club fair is held at the start of the academic year — attend it
- Most clubs have LINE groups — get added during the fair or ask a senior
- Some clubs have small annual dues; most are free
- You can join multiple clubs — there's no formal limit

---

## Starting a New Club

If your interest isn't represented:
- Bring a proposal to the Student Council
- Need a minimum number of interested members (⚠️ confirm exact number with student council)
- Faculty advisor may be required for formal recognition
