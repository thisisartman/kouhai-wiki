IUJ's student body is small and international. Clubs are the fastest way to build a real social network and find people outside your immediate program cohort.

> 💡 Club coordinators and contact emails change every year. For the current list and how to reach a specific club, check the official listing or ask at the club fair rather than relying on any name printed here indefinitely.

---

## The HOUSE Program

Before individual clubs: this is the bigger structure most social life at IUJ runs through. **8 HOUSEs**, each with a captain and coordinated by a "Head of House," compete in 3–4 events per term for points toward a HOUSE Champion crowned at year's end. HOUSE stands for "Honoring Our Unique Social Environment," and the whole program is named after Sohei Nakayama, IUJ's founder. The 8 HOUSEs are named after IUJ founding fathers/chairmen, each with a mascot/color: Sasaki (Snow Monkeys, white), Doko (Elephants, black), Nagano (Giant Pandas, orange), Mizukami (Wolves, brown), Sugihara (Bengal Tigers, purple), Okamura (Lions, green), Kobayashi (Blue Jaguars), and Yama-Ushio, a combined house (Cobras, yellow).

## Why Join a Club

- IUJ is isolated, so clubs structure your social life in a way that passively attending class doesn't
- Cross-program mixing: MBA and IR students tend to cluster separately in class; clubs break this
- Resume value: leadership roles in student orgs are legitimate for early-career profiles
- Vacation planning: sports and travel clubs often organize group trips, cheaper and more fun than going alone
- Many clubs are involved in the wiki expansion process (see [[10_Travel & Leisure/Vacation Planning/How to Plan & Budget|Vacation Planning — How to Plan & Budget]])

## How to Join

- Club fair is held at the start of the academic year; attend it
- Most clubs have LINE groups. Get added during the fair or ask a senior.
- Some clubs have small annual dues; most are free
- You can join multiple clubs, since there's no formal limit

## Starting a New Club

Per IUJ's own official guidance: **"There is no stated policy or guidelines for starting a club at IUJ. That means it is really easy."** No minimum member count, no faculty advisor requirement, just:

1. Tell **GSO-EC** (Graduate Student Organization Executive Committee) and **OSS** you intend to start a club. OSS will add it to the official Club Listings.
2. Make a poster: what the club is, what's required of members (fees? weekly commitment? one-off?), and the first meeting time/place. Give it at least 3 days before the meeting. Post on dorm bulletin boards and the brown boards outside OSS, and add it to the @iuj page and events calendar.
3. **Personally invite people.** A poster alone rarely gets a group together.
4. Don't mass-email the whole IUJ list to advertise. Invite a few people directly by email instead; a campus-wide blast isn't appropriate.
5. If you need a budget, contact GSO-EC. Requests are usually taken mid-Winter Term (fiscal year runs April 1–March 31); they sometimes have extra funds to help a new club get started, but plenty is possible without money too.
