If you want to drive in Japan beyond what your IDP covers (or if your country's IDP isn't valid in Japan), you need a Japanese driving licence. This article covers both the conversion process and getting a fresh licence from scratch.

> ⚠️ **JICA and JDS-sponsored students are not permitted to drive in Japan at all**, according to an official IUJ/OSS notice on driving rules — this applies regardless of licence or IDP status. If you're on one of these scholarships, none of the below applies to you until that restriction changes.

---

## Do You Need This?

**You don't need this if:**
- Your home country's IDP is valid in Japan AND you plan to leave within 1 year
- You don't plan to drive at all

**You do need this if:**
- Your home country's IDP is NOT recognised in Japan (e.g. USA, Germany, France, Belgium, Switzerland)
- Your IDP is about to expire and you're staying longer
- You want a permanent Japanese licence

---

## Part 1: Converting a Foreign Licence

Japan has a conversion process (切り替え, *kirikae*) for foreign licence holders. The process and difficulty vary significantly by country.

### Countries with Simplified Conversion (Short Process)

Countries that have reciprocal agreements with Japan — includes most European countries (excluding the exceptions above), Australia, New Zealand, Canada, South Korea, Taiwan, and others.

**Process:**
1. Gather documents (see below)
2. Visit the **Niigata Driving Licence Centre (新潟県運転免許センター)** in Niigata City
3. Take a **Japanese knowledge test** (translated; 10 questions; straightforward if you review)
4. Take an **eyesight test**
5. Pay the fee (~¥4,000–5,000 total)
6. Licence issued same day if you pass

**Documents needed:**
- Current foreign driving licence (original)
- Certified Japanese translation of your foreign licence (from JAF — Japan Automobile Federation — or the respective embassy)
- Passport
- Residence Card (在留カード)
- My Number Card or notification slip
- Passport photos (3–4, check current requirements)
- Application form (fill in at the centre)

> 💡 Get your JAF translation before going to the centre: [jaf.or.jp/e/switch](https://www.jaf.or.jp/e/switch). Takes 1–2 weeks. Cost: ~¥3,000.

### Countries Requiring Full Practical Test

Some countries (notably the USA and certain others) require applicants to take a practical driving test in addition to the written test — despite being licensed drivers. This is a bureaucratic quirk, not a comment on your driving ability.

**Additional steps:**
- Book a practical test date at the Niigata Driving Licence Centre
- Test is conducted in Japanese; an interpreter may be allowed (confirm in advance)
- The test is notoriously precise — study Japan-specific manoeuvres (mirror-checking sequence, speed, positioning)
- Many applicants fail once or twice; this is normal; budget time for retakes

## Part 2: Getting a Fresh Japanese Licence

If you have no driving licence at all and want one in Japan:

### Option A: Driving School (教習所, *Kyōshūjo*)

The standard path. You attend a licensed driving school, complete required classroom hours and practical lessons, then take the final test at the licence centre.

**Timeline:** 1–3 months depending on your schedule and the school's availability

**Cost:** ¥200,000–350,000 for a full course (manual or automatic)

**Nearest driving schools:** In Nagaoka. Ask seniors for which school has worked for IUJ international students — English support varies.

**Automatic vs Manual:** Automatic (AT限定) is cheaper and faster. If you want to drive manual, specify this — the licence will be unrestricted.

### Option B: Direct Test at the Licence Centre (一発試験)

Take the knowledge and practical test without attending a driving school. Much cheaper but significantly harder — the practical test at the centre is more demanding than the school's internal test.

Used by experienced drivers who don't want to pay for school. Budget for 2–4 attempts.

---

## The Niigata Driving Licence Centre

**Location:** 新潟県新潟市中央区紫竹山 (Niigata City, Chūō-ku, Shichikuyama)
**Access:** Bus from Niigata Station; ~30 min

Bring all documents. Arrive early — centres process applicants in batches and missing your slot means rescheduling.

---

## Timeline Advice

- **IDP holders:** Start the conversion process before your IDP's 1-year mark — don't wait until the last minute
- **Fresh licence:** If you want one before winter (for car access), start at the driving school in September or October of your first term
- **Simplified conversion track:** Some countries qualify for a simplified process — JAF translation + knowledge test + eyesight test, often completed in one day. Check whether your home country is on the list before booking your appointment.
