Japan's waste disposal system is strict, detailed, and non-negotiable. Putting the wrong rubbish in the wrong bin causes real problems — Minami-Uonuma City will refuse to collect a wrongly-sorted bag, and it becomes IUJ's problem to fix. Learn the system in the first week.

---

## IUJ Dorm Rules (SD1, SD2, SD3, MSA) — the rules that actually apply to you

This is IUJ's own garbage guide (Office of General Affairs, effective September 2025), not a generic Japan framework — follow this over any general assumption about plastic/PET sorting.

**Burnable vs. Unburnable** — two bins in your room, plus a shared bucket in the garbage room:

| Burnable | Unburnable |
|---|---|
| Everything else, **including plastic packaging and PET bottles** | Cans, glass bottles, pottery, small batteries (within 15cm) |

> ⚠️ **Plastic packaging and PET bottles go in BURNABLE at IUJ dorms** — they are *not* a separate sorted category here, unlike the generic Japan system described further below. Mixing this up (or putting cans/glass in burnable) means the city refuses collection and IUJ has to redo the sorting.

**Recyclable paper** — separate into 4 bundles, tied with string, left in the garbage room:
- Corrugated cardboard (only)
- Newspapers (only, no leaflets)
- Milk/juice cartons (rinse, open, fold, tie)
- Everything else — leaflets, magazines, books, copy paper (fold and tie)

Paper with metal or plastic parts attached can't be recycled this way.

**Big items** — report to Dorm Staff *before* disposing of anything large, and follow their instructions; unreported items won't be collected. Sizes are ticketed:

| Size | Examples | Ticket cost |
|---|---|---|
| Small | Skis, snowboards, umbrellas, cushions, lamps, printers, small pots/pans | ¥500 |
| Middle | Tatami, futons, chairs, microwaves, bicycles, snow shovels | ¥1,000 |
| Large | Beds (non-spring), sofas (2+ seats), dryers | ¥2,000 |

Attach the ticket to the item and hand it to Dorm Staff (small) or leave it at their designated spot/time (middle/large).

- **Free "Special Service Period for Big Items"**: the week before and after graduation each June — IUJ takes big items (except the 4 specified appliances below) for free during this window.
- **Car tires and car batteries** aren't covered by Minami-Uonuma City's waste system at all — take them to a gas station (not free).
- **4 specified home appliances** — air conditioner, TV, fridge/freezer, washing machine/dryer — are never treated as "big item" waste, even during the free period. Disposing of one costs a flat **¥4,000 IUJ disposal charge** (ask Dorm Staff), or return it to the shop you bought it from if that's an option.

## General Japan Framework (background, not IUJ-specific)

Categories and collection days vary by municipality — the table below is the general Japan pattern often referenced online, but **defer to the IUJ dorm rules above where they differ** (as they do for plastic/PET).

| Category | Japanese | What Goes In |
|---|---|---|
| Combustible / Burnable | 燃えるごみ (*moeru gomi*) | Food scraps, paper, wood, small plastic items |
| Non-combustible | 燃えないごみ (*moenai gomi*) | Metal, glass, ceramics, rubber, small appliances |
| Plastic packaging | プラスチック (*purasuchikku*) | Clean plastic packaging with the プラ symbol — **at IUJ dorms this goes in Burnable instead, see above** |
| PET bottles | ペットボトル | Rinsed plastic bottles — **at IUJ dorms this goes in Burnable instead, see above** |
| Cans | 缶 (*kan*) | Rinsed aluminium and steel cans |
| Glass bottles | ビン (*bin*) | Rinsed glass bottles, sorted by colour in some areas |
| Cardboard / Paper | 段ボール / 紙類 | Flattened, tied in bundles |
| Large items (粗大ごみ) | 粗大ごみ (*sodai gomi*) | Furniture, large appliances — special collection required |

## Required Bags

Many municipalities require waste to be placed in designated official bags (指定ごみ袋), sold at supermarkets and convenience stores. These are usually colour-coded by category.

> ⚠️ Confirm which bags are required for Minami-Uonuma City specifically. Ask at the dorm office or check with the city hall.

Collection happens on specific days for specific categories.

## Key Rules

- **Rinse** all food containers before sorting
- **Flatten** cardboard and tie with string
- **Remove caps and labels** from PET bottles (cap in plastic, label in plastic or paper depending on material)
- **Do not leave bags early** — put waste out on the morning of collection, not the night before (animals can get into it)
- **No mixing** — a contaminated bag may be left uncollected with a sticker explaining why
