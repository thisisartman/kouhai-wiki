# 🗑️ Waste Disposal & Recycling

Japan's waste disposal system is strict, detailed, and non-negotiable. Putting the wrong rubbish in the wrong bag on the wrong day causes real problems — complaints from neighbours, dorm management notices, and fines in some cases. Learn the system in the first week.

> ⚠️ Minami-Uonuma City's specific rules and collection schedule must be verified against current municipal guidelines. IUJ may also have dorm-specific rules. The framework below is accurate for most Japanese municipalities — verify the specifics on-site.

---

## The Core Principle

Japan sorts waste into categories that must be separated before disposal. Each category goes in a specific bag, on a specific collection day, at a specific location.

The categories and collection days vary by municipality — Minami-Uonuma City has its own schedule. IUJ's dormitory management should provide a waste disposal guide on arrival.

---

## Typical Waste Categories in Japan

| Category | Japanese | What Goes In |
|---|---|---|
| Combustible / Burnable | 燃えるごみ (*moeru gomi*) | Food scraps, paper, wood, small plastic items |
| Non-combustible | 燃えないごみ (*moenai gomi*) | Metal, glass, ceramics, rubber, small appliances |
| Plastic packaging | プラスチック (*purasuchikku*) | Clean plastic packaging with the プラ symbol |
| PET bottles | ペットボトル | Rinsed plastic bottles, cap and label removed |
| Cans | 缶 (*kan*) | Rinsed aluminium and steel cans |
| Glass bottles | ビン (*bin*) | Rinsed glass bottles, sorted by colour in some areas |
| Cardboard / Paper | 段ボール / 紙類 | Flattened, tied in bundles |
| Large items (粗大ごみ) | 粗大ごみ (*sodai gomi*) | Furniture, large appliances — special collection required |

---

## Required Bags

Many municipalities require waste to be placed in designated official bags (指定ごみ袋), sold at supermarkets and convenience stores. These are usually colour-coded by category.

> ⚠️ Confirm which bags are required for Minami-Uonuma City. Ask at the dorm office or check with the city hall.

---

## Collection Schedule

Collection happens on specific days for specific categories. A typical week might look like:

- Monday/Thursday: Combustible waste
- Tuesday: Plastic packaging
- Wednesday: Cans, PET bottles
- (varies)

The IUJ dorm should have this schedule posted. If not, the Minami-Uonuma City website (南魚沼市) has the official schedule.

---

## Large Item Disposal (粗大ごみ)

You cannot put furniture, appliances, or large items in regular rubbish bags. Options:

1. **Book a municipal collection** — call or apply online to Minami-Uonuma City; a fee applies
2. **Sell or give away** — Mercari, student community, or recycle shops
3. **Take to a recycling centre** — ask at the dorm office for the nearest facility

> 💡 When you move out, dispose of large items well in advance. Last-minute large item disposal is a common end-of-year scramble.

---

## Key Rules

- **Rinse** all food containers before sorting
- **Flatten** cardboard and tie with string
- **Remove caps and labels** from PET bottles (cap in plastic, label in plastic or paper depending on material)
- **Do not leave bags early** — put waste out on the morning of collection, not the night before (animals can get into it)
- **No mixing** — a contaminated bag may be left uncollected with a sticker explaining why
