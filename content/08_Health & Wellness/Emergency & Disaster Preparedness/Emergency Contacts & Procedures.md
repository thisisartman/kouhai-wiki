**Calling 119 (official IUJ script):**
- Dial **119** from a mobile phone, or **0-119** from a campus/campus-line phone
- If you don't speak Japanese, ask the Dorm Director (ext. 555) or your Floor Representative to call for you
- What to say:
  1. "Kyūkyū desu. Kyūkyūsha o onegai shimasu." (It's an emergency. Please send an ambulance.)
  2. "Kokusai Daigaku Gakuseiryō [Dorm Name] no [Room Number] gōshitsu desu." (IUJ student dormitory, [Dorm], Room [Number] — dorm name is SD1/SD2/SD3/MSA)
- Stay on the line

## Medical Emergency

1. **Call 119** for serious injury, unconsciousness, severe chest pain, difficulty breathing, suspected stroke
2. **Do not move** someone with a potential spinal injury
3. Ambulance (救急車) service is **free** — no charges for the ride
4. You'll typically be taken to **Uonuma Kikan Hospital** (025-777-3200) — IUJ's own emergency-care guide centers entirely on this hospital, ~10 minutes from campus. It does not route students to Nagaoka.
5. Bring your NHI card (健康保険証) if possible — but treatment will not be refused without it in an emergency
6. **Two AEDs are on campus** if needed before help arrives: at the SD1 entrance, and in the guard's room near the main entrance. Instructions are on the devices in English and Japanese.
7. If urgent (but not ambulance-level) care is needed at night or on a holiday, you can call and visit on your own: Uonuma Kikan Hospital (025-777-3200) or Minami Uonuma City Hospital (025-788-1222).
8. **If hospitalization is needed**, you'll be asked for two guarantors: an Identity Guarantor (you, the student) and a Joint Guarantor (a person — often family — who guarantees payment). Worth arranging who this would be in advance rather than scrambling for it during an emergency.

**At the hospital without Japanese:**
- Show your NHI card
- Use Google Translate's camera feature for forms
- Many hospitals have a basic translation service or access to telephone interpreters — ask

## Fire

1. **Alert others** and evacuate immediately — do not wait to collect belongings
2. **Call 119** once outside
3. IUJ dormitories have fire evacuation plans posted in hallways — find your nearest exit on your first day
4. Fire extinguishers are red and wall-mounted — know where the nearest one is to your room

## Crime / Police

- **Call 110** for crimes in progress, accidents, or emergencies requiring police
- **For non-emergency:** visit the nearest koban (交番, police box) — there is one near Urasa station
- **For lost or stolen items:** go to the koban with your details. Japan has a strong lost-and-found culture — items are frequently returned.
- **Lost Residence Card:** Report immediately to the nearest Immigration Services Bureau or IUJ student affairs — legally required within 14 days of losing it

## Mental Health Crisis

If you or someone else is in a mental health crisis:
- **TELL Lifeline (English):** 03-5774-0992
- **Inochi no Denwa (24/7, Japanese):** 0120-783-556
- **IUJ Health Centre:** During office hours
- Do not leave someone alone if you are seriously concerned about their safety — stay with them and call for help
