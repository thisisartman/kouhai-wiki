Registering with the city is legally required within 14 days of arrival. IUJ organizes this as an on-campus session: city office staff come to IUJ to process it, so you don't need to travel to the city office yourself. Don't skip it.

---

## Why It Matters

- **Required by law**: all foreign residents must register their address with the local municipality
- Unlocks access to: National Health Insurance, My Number Card, and various government services. (Pension exemption is separate; see note below.)
- Your registered address appears on your Residence Card
- Without registration, you cannot open a bank account or access public services

---

## Where to Go

**On campus, not the city office.** Minami-Uonuma City Office (南魚沼市役所) staff visit IUJ for a dedicated new-student registration session. IUJ will announce the date and location on campus. You register, enroll in health insurance, and get your My Number notification there, without a trip into town.

> 🔶 Senior input needed: exact on-campus location/building for this session, and whether a city-office trip is ever still required for anyone (e.g. those who arrive outside the main intake window).

---

## What to Do in One Visit

Combine these in the same on-campus session; they're handled by different counters, set up side by side for the day:

1. ☑️ **Residence Registration (住民登録)**: register your address
2. ☑️ **National Health Insurance (国民健康保険) enrollment**: sign up immediately after registering
3. ☑️ **My Number notification (マイナンバー)**: you'll receive your number; card application is separate

Tell the first counter you need to do all of them.

> ⚠️ **Don't try to apply for pension exemption here.** IUJ submits your pension exemption automatically through a separate mandatory event called Tax & Pension Day, held later in the year. See [[Pension Exemption — How to Apply]] for the real process.

---

## What to Bring

- Passport
- Residence Card (在留カード)
- IUJ enrollment certificate (在学証明書), for insurance enrollment
- Personal seal (印鑑) if you have one; signature usually accepted for foreigners
- Any scholarship documentation (for income-based insurance premium calculation)

> 💡 Bring more documents than you think you need. Japanese administrative offices prefer over-documentation.

---

## National Health Insurance (国民健康保険)

- Enrollment is **mandatory** for all foreign residents staying over 3 months
- Premium is income-based: as a student with low/no income, your premium will be minimal (~¥1,000–3,000/month)
- Covers 70% of medical costs at enrolled clinics and hospitals
- Your insurance card (保険証) arrives by mail within 1–2 weeks

> ⚠️ Don't go to a clinic before you have your insurance card unless it's an emergency. Without it, you pay 100% upfront (though reimbursement is possible afterward).

---

## Updating Your Registration

Any time your address changes (room change, moving off-campus):
- Return to the city office within 14 days
- Bring Residence Card and new address details
- Free, takes ~10 minutes

---

## English Support

- City office staff generally do not speak English, whether they're visiting IUJ for the initial session or you're at the actual city office for a later update
- IUJ may provide a support letter, or have a staff member on hand during the on-campus session
- Bring Google Translate or a translation app ready on your phone
- Forms are in Japanese, but IUJ seniors or staff can help pre-fill them

## Useful Japanese for the City Office

| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 住民登録をしたいです | Jūmin tōroku o shitai desu | I'd like to register my residence |
| 健康保険に加入したいです | Kenkō hoken ni kanyū shitai desu | I'd like to enroll in health insurance |
| 学生です | Gakusei desu | I am a student |
| IUJの学生です | IUJ no gakusei desu | I am an IUJ student |

Pension exemption has its own phrase; see [[Pension Exemption — How to Apply]], since that's a different visit entirely.
