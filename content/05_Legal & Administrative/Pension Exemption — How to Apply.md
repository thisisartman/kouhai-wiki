Japan requires all residents aged 20–59 to enroll in the national pension system (国民年金, *kokumin nenkin*). Students qualify for a **full exemption** from monthly payments. Most new arrivals don't know to apply — don't miss this.

---

## Why This Matters

- Monthly pension premium: **¥16,980/month** (2024 rate)
- As a student, you can get this reduced to **¥0** via the student exemption
- Without applying, you will be billed and the debt accumulates
- The exemption period still counts toward your pension record — you're not losing entitlement, just deferring payment

> ⚠️ This is not automatic. You have to apply. Do it at the city office on your first visit.

---

## Student Exemption vs Full Exemption

There are two relevant programs:

| Program | Who Qualifies | Reduction |
|---|---|---|
| **Student Special Payment System (学生納付特例)** | Enrolled students under 50 | Full exemption (¥0/month) |
| **Income-Based Full Exemption (全額免除)** | Low-income non-students | Full exemption |

As an IUJ student, you want the **Student Special Payment System (学生納付特例制度)**.

---

## How to Apply

### At the City Office (Recommended — Do This on First Visit)

1. Go to the pension/national insurance counter (国民年金担当窓口)
2. Say: **「学生納付特例の申請をしたいです」** (*Gakusei nōfu tokurei no shinsei o shitai desu*)
3. Fill in the application form

**What to bring:**
- Residence Card
- My Number (notification letter or card)
- IUJ enrollment certificate (在学証明書) — get from IUJ admin office **before** going
- Pension book (年金手帳) if you already have one (unlikely for first-timers)

### Online

- Can also apply via マイナポータル if you have a My Number Card
- Requires digital certificate on card — more complex; city office visit is easier

---

## Exemption Period & Renewal

- Exemption covers April of the current year to March of the next (Japanese fiscal year)
- **Renew every year** — it doesn't automatically continue
- IUJ may send reminders, but don't rely on it; set a calendar reminder for February/March each year
- If your enrollment certificate covers multiple years, ask if multi-year application is possible

---

## What Happens After Graduation

You have **10 years** after the exemption period to pay back the deferred premiums if you want full pension credit. If you never pay them back, those months count at a reduced rate in your final pension calculation.

For most international students who will not retire in Japan, this is not a practical concern.
