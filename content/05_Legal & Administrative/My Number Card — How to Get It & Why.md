My Number is Japan's national identification number system. Every resident — including foreign nationals — gets a number. The physical card that goes with it is optional but increasingly essential.

---

## My Number vs My Number Card

| | My Number (番号) | My Number Card (カード) |
|---|---|---|
| What it is | A 12-digit individual ID number | Physical IC card with your photo |
| When you get it | Automatically after city registration | Only if you apply for it |
| What it's used for | Tax filing, insurance, official records | ID, digital authentication, convenience store printing, medical records |
| Required? | Yes (number itself) | Technically optional, but increasingly necessary |

---

## How You Get Your Number

After registering at the city office:
- A notification letter (通知カード or マイナンバー通知書) is mailed to your dorm address within 1–2 weeks
- This letter contains your 12-digit My Number
- **Keep this letter safe** — it's your official number record until you get the physical card

---

## Why You Should Get the Physical Card

Even though it's technically optional, get the card. Here's why:

- **Tax filing** — required for e-Tax (online tax filing); increasingly mandatory
- **Banking** — some banks now require My Number for account operations
- **Health insurance** — My Number Card is replacing the traditional health insurance card (保険証) — Japan is phasing this in
- **Convenience store printing** — My Number Card + convenience store terminals lets you print official government documents (住民票, etc.) without going to city hall
- **Digital government services** — needed for the マイナポータル (My Number Portal) app for various administrative tasks
- **Student scholarships** — some scholarship processes require it

---

## How to Apply for the Physical Card

### Step 1: Get your notification letter
After city registration, wait for the letter at your dorm address (~1–2 weeks).

### Step 2: Apply

**Option A: Online (easiest)**
1. Go to [mynumbercard.point.soumu.go.jp](https://mynumbercard.point.soumu.go.jp)
2. Use the QR code on your notification letter to access the application
3. Upload a smartphone photo of your face (plain background, no glasses)
4. Submit

**Option B: At the city office**
1. Bring notification letter + Residence Card + passport
2. Fill in the application form at the counter
3. Staff take your photo on-site

### Step 3: Pick up the card
- Notification arrives by mail in ~4–8 weeks
- Bring the pickup notice, Residence Card, and passport to the city office
- You'll set a 4-digit PIN at pickup

---

## My Number Portal App (マイナポータル)

Once you have the card:
- Download マイナポータル app
- Link your health insurance, pension records, and tax history
- Check and manage various government procedures from your phone
- Requires NFC-capable smartphone to read the card

---

## Giving Your Number to Employers

If you work part-time, employers will ask for your My Number for tax withholding purposes. You're legally required to provide it. You can give:
- The physical My Number Card, or
- The notification letter + Residence Card as a combined set

---

## Privacy Note

My Number is sensitive. Do not share it unnecessarily. Legitimate uses are: tax, insurance, employment, and official government services. No shop or private business should ask for it.
