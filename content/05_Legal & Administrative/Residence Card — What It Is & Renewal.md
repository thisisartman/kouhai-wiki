# 🪪 Residence Card (在留カード) — What It Is & Renewal

Your Residence Card (在留カード, *zairyū kādo*) is your single most important document in Japan. More immediately useful than your passport for day-to-day legal identity.

---

## What It Is

- Issued by the Japanese government to all mid-to-long-term foreign residents
- Confirms your legal status, visa type, and permitted activities in Japan
- **Required by law to carry at all times** — police can ask to see it
- Serves as ID for: bank account opening, SIM registration, city hall procedures, apartment contracts, and more

---

## What's on It

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| Name | As on passport |
| Date of birth | — |
| Nationality | — |
| Address | Your registered Japanese address |
| Status of residence | e.g., "College Student" |
| Period of stay | Expiry date of your current visa |
| Permission to work | Back of card — 資格外活動許可 stamp if you can work |

> ⚠️ The address on your card must always be current. If you move rooms within IUJ or move out, update it at the city office within **14 days**.

---

## When You Receive It

- If flying into a major airport (NRT, HND, KIX): issued **at the airport** on arrival
- If arriving at a smaller port of entry: you receive a passport stamp; the physical card arrives by mail within 2 weeks

---

## Always Carry It

This is not optional. Japanese law (Immigration Control Act) requires foreign nationals to carry it at all times. Failure to present it when asked by authorities is a fineable offence.

Practical tip: keep it in your wallet, not your room.

---

## Updating Your Address

Any time your address changes:
1. Go to Minami-Uonuma City Office (南魚沼市役所)
2. Bring your Residence Card
3. Request address update (住所変更, *jūsho henkō*)
4. The new address is printed on the back of the card

Must be done within **14 days** of moving.

---

## Renewal

Your Residence Card expires when your visa status expires (usually tied to your program length + a buffer).

**When to renew:**
- Apply for an extension **3 months before expiry** — don't wait until the last minute
- If your program length changes (e.g., thesis extension), renew accordingly

**Where to renew:**
- **Nagaoka Regional Immigration Services Bureau** (nearest office)
  - Address: confirm current address at [immi-moj.go.jp](https://www.moj.go.jp/isa/)
  - Or apply online via the Immigration Bureau's web system for eligible categories

**What to bring for renewal:**
- Current Residence Card
- Passport
- Enrollment certificate (在学証明書) from IUJ — get from admin office
- Completed application form (available at the immigration office or printable online)
- Photographs (4cm × 3cm, plain background)
- Fee: ¥4,000 revenue stamp (収入印紙) — sold at post offices and convenience stores

**Processing time:** ~2 weeks to 2 months depending on workload. Apply early.

---

## If Lost or Stolen

1. Report to the nearest police station immediately — get a 紛失届 (loss report)
2. Go to the nearest Regional Immigration Services Bureau with:
   - Passport
   - Police report
   - 2 passport photos
3. Reissuance fee: ¥1,600

> ⚠️ Do not delay reporting a lost card. Operating without one is an offence, even if you've just lost it.
