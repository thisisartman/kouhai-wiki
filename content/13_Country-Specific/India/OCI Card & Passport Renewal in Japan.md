Two documents that Indian students at IUJ may need to manage while in Japan: the OCI (Overseas Citizen of India) card and passport renewal. Both are handled through the Indian Embassy in Tokyo or the Consulates in Osaka and other cities.

---

## OCI Card

### What Is It?

The OCI (Overseas Citizen of India) card is a lifelong visa for foreign nationals of Indian origin (or those who were Indian citizens, or whose parents/grandparents were Indian citizens). It allows multiple-entry, multi-purpose, lifelong visa to India and certain other benefits.

**If you don't have one:** You can apply while in Japan. This is worth doing if you're eligible — it simplifies future travel to India significantly.

**If you have one:** Maintain it. When you renew your passport, the OCI card must be re-issued or cross-referenced (a booklet update is required when the passport it's linked to expires).

---

## OCI Application/Renewal in Japan

Applications are handled online through the Indian government's OCI portal, then processed through the Indian Embassy in Tokyo.

**Embassy of India, Tokyo:**
- Address: 2-2-11 Kudan Minami, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo 102-0074
- Website: [indembassy-tokyo.gov.in](https://www.indembassy-tokyo.gov.in)
- Consular services: Book appointment online; check current processing times

**Indian Consulate, Osaka:**
- Covers Niigata and much of central/western Japan
- May be more relevant than Tokyo for some procedures; confirm jurisdiction with the embassy

### OCI Application Process (Simplified)
1. Apply online at [ociservices.gov.in](https://ociservices.gov.in)
2. Pay the fee online (~US$275 for new; less for re-issuance)
3. Upload required documents (passport, photos, existing OCI if applicable, relationship proof if applying on family connection)
4. Mail physical documents to the Indian Embassy in Tokyo or attend in person
5. Processing: 4–8 weeks typically; varies

> ⚠️ If your OCI is linked to an old passport (now expired or different), you need to update it. This is called "OCI booklet re-issuance," and the fee is lower with a simpler process.

## Passport Renewal in Japan

Indian passports are valid for 10 years (adult). If yours is expiring during or shortly after your IUJ programme, renew it in Japan rather than waiting to return home.

### Why Renew in Japan?

- You need a valid passport for all travel and residence purposes
- Tatkal service in India requires being physically present; from Japan, you apply through the embassy
- Renewing in Japan avoids a trip home mid-programme

### The Process

1. **Check eligibility and requirements** on the Indian Embassy Tokyo website, since requirements change
2. **Apply online** at [passportindia.gov.in](https://passportindia.gov.in) → select Passport Seva Kendra abroad → Tokyo
3. **Book an appointment** at the Embassy in Tokyo
4. **Documents typically required:**
   - Current passport (original + copy)
   - Completed application form
   - Recent passport photographs (per Indian specs — different from Japanese; 2"×2", white background)
   - Residence Card (in lieu of Indian address proof while abroad)
   - Any additional documents requested

5. **Attend appointment** in Tokyo. The embassy is near Kudanshita Station (Hanzomon line).
6. **Processing:** 3–6 weeks for normal; express service may be available, so check current status

**Cost:** ~¥5,000–8,000 equivalent depending on service type (normal/Tatkal)

### Practical Notes

- **Plan 3–4 months ahead.** Don't let your passport expire while in Japan.
- Tokyo is 1.5h from IUJ; embassy appointments are typically on weekdays. Plan a Tokyo trip around it.
- The embassy's website is the authoritative source — check it, as procedures change
- If your passport expires while your residence card is still valid, inform IUJ student affairs immediately
