India-specific items to sort out before you leave. These are layered on top of the general [[Visa & Documentation]] and [[Packing List]] checklists.

---

## Documents

- [ ] **Passport.** Verify expiry; minimum 6 months beyond your program end date. Tatkal renewal is possible but stressful. Do this early.
- [ ] **CoE (Certificate of Eligibility)**, issued by IUJ and delivered by post. Confirm delivery address is accurate and reachable.
- [ ] **Japanese Student Visa:** apply at the Japanese Embassy or Consulate in your city (Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Bengaluru all have consulates/embassy)
- [ ] **PAN Card + Aadhaar**, needed for Indian banking/tax purposes while you're abroad. Don't leave them in India.
- [ ] **Scholarship letter:** MEXT, JASSO, or private scholarship documentation

> 💡 The Japanese Embassy in India typically processes student visas in 3–5 working days. Book your appointment online at [in.emb-japan.go.jp](https://www.in.emb-japan.go.jp).

## Banking & Finance

- [ ] **Notify your Indian bank** of international travel, or your cards may get blocked on first foreign use
- [ ] **Enable international transactions** on your debit/credit card (SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis all have this in their apps, or it may require a branch visit)
- [ ] **Set up a Wise account.** See [[Remittances & Forex Setup]] for full guidance.
- [ ] **Get a forex card** if you prefer; Niyo Global or HDFC Forex Card are popular choices
- [ ] **Withdraw ¥20,000–30,000** in yen cash and exchange it at your bank in India (better rates than airports); order in advance
- [ ] **Note your Indian bank's SWIFT code** and international transfer details for future remittances
- [ ] **Keep your UPI apps active** (BHIM, PhonePe, GPay). You can still send/receive money in India from Japan, and they're useful when you visit home.

## Health & Insurance

- [ ] **Health check-up.** IUJ may require or request a medical certificate. Get this done before departure.
- [ ] **Dental work.** Japan's dental care is good but expensive without insurance. Sort any pending work in India.
- [ ] **Prescription medications:** 3-month supply maximum without a Yakkan Shomei permit. See [[Visa & Documentation]].
- [ ] **Prescription records**, printed copies in English, stamped by doctor
- [ ] **Vaccinations.** Check Japan's current entry requirements; routine vaccines (Hepatitis A/B, Typhoid, Tetanus) are advisable.

---

## Communication Setup

- [ ] **Buy an unlocked phone** or confirm your existing phone is unlockable before departure. Many Indian phones (especially Jio devices) are carrier-locked.
- [ ] **Back up your Indian SIM contacts**, since you may use a Japanese SIM as primary in Japan
- [ ] **Set up Google Voice / WhatsApp Business** or any secondary number app if you need to keep an Indian number active
- [ ] **Inform family of contact plan.** Tell them whether you'll be reachable on WhatsApp, email, or your current Indian number during travel.

## Indian Food & Essentials

Rural Niigata has almost no South Asian grocery options. Nagaoka has limited options. Bring:

- [ ] **Spice kit:** turmeric, cumin seeds, coriander powder, red chili powder, garam masala, mustard seeds, dried methi, small Ziploc bags each. This is the most useful thing you can bring.
- [ ] **Atta / wheat flour**, if you prefer rotis; rice flour too if applicable. Bring enough for the first month.
- [ ] **Pickle.** One or two jars of your preferred achar. Japan has no equivalent.
- [ ] **Dried lentils (dal).** Japanese supermarkets don't stock toor dal or moong dal; bring 1–2 kg.
- [ ] **Instant mixes**: idli/dosa mix, khichdi, upma, etc. for homesick nights
- [ ] **Papadums / papads**, which store well and are unavailable in Nagaoka

> 💡 These will feel heavy in your luggage and essential within 2 weeks of arriving. Other Indian students will thank you when you share.

For ongoing Indian food sourcing in Japan, see [[Indian Food Sources]].

## Academic / IUJ Prep

- [ ] **Review IUJ pre-arrival information packet.** IUJ sends this; read it fully.
- [ ] **Sort out your laptop.** IUJ work is laptop-intensive; if yours is ageing, upgrade before leaving India (significantly cheaper than Japan).
- [ ] **Download offline resources**: maps, translation apps, Navitime, Google Translate (with Japanese downloaded for offline use)
- [ ] **MEXT/JASSO students:** confirm stipend payment schedule and which bank account it goes to

---

## Logistics

- [ ] **Inform your Residential Society / landlord** if relevant
- [ ] **Set up mail forwarding or a trusted person** to receive Indian post in your absence
- [ ] **File income tax return** (ITR) if due. Easier to do before you leave, but can also be filed from Japan.
- [ ] **Check if you need to update your address** with Indian banks, Aadhaar, or PAN for NRI/non-resident status. Consult a CA if unsure.
- [ ] **Arrange for someone to handle India-side banking** (fixed deposits, SIPs, etc.) if needed

---

## Travel Day

- [ ] **Flight booking.** Prefer Haneda (HND) over Narita (NRT) if possible; it's easier to get in from.
- [ ] **Confirm Shinkansen route** from Tokyo to Urasa before you land. See [[Airport to IUJ Routes]].
- [ ] **Save IUJ's address in Japanese** on your phone for taxi drivers: `新潟県南魚沼市大崎1番地 国際大学`
- [ ] **Screenshot the Urasa Station campus bus schedule**, since it doesn't run all day. See [[Urasa Station — Full Guide & Quirks]].
- [ ] **Tell someone at home** your flight details and expected arrival time at IUJ
