Getting to Japan legally is a multi-step process that starts months before you arrive. The core bottleneck is the Certificate of Eligibility (CoE). Everything else depends on it. Start early.

---

## The Certificate of Eligibility (CoE)

The CoE is issued by Japanese Immigration and is the document that proves you're eligible for a student visa. IUJ handles the application on your behalf after you accept your offer.

**What you need to do:**
- Submit all required documents to IUJ's admissions/international office promptly after accepting
- Do NOT make flight bookings until you have the CoE in hand. Processing takes 4–8 weeks and delays are common
- Once IUJ sends you the CoE (by post or courier), take it to your nearest Japanese Embassy or Consulate

> ⚠️ The CoE has a validity period. Apply for your visa and book travel promptly after receiving it, typically within 3 months.

---

## Visa Application at the Embassy/Consulate

**What to bring:**
- Certificate of Eligibility (original)
- Valid passport (at least 6 months validity beyond your intended stay)
- Visa application form (download from your local Japanese Embassy website)
- Passport-sized photos (usually 2, check specific embassy requirements)
- Any additional documents your embassy requires (varies by country)

**Processing time:** Usually 3–5 business days after submission. Some embassies offer same-day or next-day service for an extra fee.

**Visa type:** You'll receive a **College Student (留学)** visa, typically valid for multiple entries with 1–2 year duration. Renew it through IUJ before it expires.

> 💡 Keep the original CoE even after getting your visa. You may need it at the port of entry.

## Documents to Gather Before Leaving

Start collecting these as early as possible. Some take weeks to obtain.

### Academic & IUJ Documents
| Document | Notes |
|---|---|
| IUJ Acceptance Letter | Required for CoE application and visa |
| CoE (Certificate of Eligibility) | Issued by IUJ; original required at embassy |
| Scholarship award letter | If applicable; needed for bank account and stipend setup |
| Academic transcripts | Keep certified copies; sometimes needed for admin |

### Identity & Travel Documents
| Document | Notes |
|---|---|
| Passport | Minimum 6 months validity; check expiry NOW |
| Visa | Affixed to passport after embassy application |
| Passport-sized photos | Bring extras (10+); needed for MyNumber, bank accounts, etc.) |
| Birth certificate | Certified copy; rarely needed but good to have |

### Financial Documents
| Document | Notes |
|---|---|
| Bank statements | Some embassies require proof of funds |
| Scholarship documentation | For banking and scholarship registration in Japan |

### Health Documents
| Document | Notes |
|---|---|
| Vaccination records | Japan may require specific vaccinations; check current requirements |
| Health insurance documents | If you have travel/international health insurance for the transit period |
| Prescription records | If you take regular medication (see notes below) |

## Medications: What to Know

Japan has strict rules about which medications can be brought in.

- **Narcotics and stimulants** (including some ADHD medications like Adderall, and certain pain medications) are **prohibited** even with a prescription. Research your specific medication before you travel.
- **Allowed prescription medications** can be brought in quantities up to a 1-month supply without a permit; up to 3 months with a **Yakkan Shomei (薬監証明)** import certificate, obtained from the Japanese Embassy before departure.
- Carry medications in original packaging with prescriptions.

> ⚠️ If you rely on a controlled substance, sort this out 2–3 months before departure. The Yakkan Shomei process takes time.

---

## Checklist: Pre-Visa

- [ ] Accept IUJ offer and submit CoE application documents
- [ ] Check passport expiry and renew if needed (this alone can take 4–6 weeks)
- [ ] Research medication rules for any prescription drugs you take
- [ ] Book embassy appointment if required (some require appointments in advance)

## Checklist: Post-CoE, Pre-Departure

- [ ] Receive CoE from IUJ
- [ ] Submit visa application at Japanese Embassy/Consulate
- [ ] Receive visa and check dates and entry conditions
- [ ] Collect all documents listed above
- [ ] Make 2–3 physical copies of every important document
- [ ] Store copies separately from originals (one set in checked luggage, one in carry-on)
