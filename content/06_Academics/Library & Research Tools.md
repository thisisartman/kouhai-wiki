## IUJ Library: Overview

The IUJ Library (国際大学図書館) is a research-grade academic library serving a small graduate school. It punches above its weight for its size: access to major international academic databases, strong IR and business collections, and relatively fast interlibrary loan.

**Location:** Main academic building on campus
**Hours:** ⚠️ Confirm current hours on the [IUJ library hours page](https://www2.iuj.ac.jp/mlic/information/hours.cfm), since hours differ during term vs. holidays. As one illustration of the pattern: for summer holiday 2026, MLIC changed opening hours starting the day after Spring term classes ended, reverting to normal hours on the first day of Fall term. Expect a similar shift around every term break — the specific hours themselves live only on that page, not in any announcement email, so check it directly each time.

## Library Card & Access

- Your IUJ student ID doubles as your library card
- Register at the library counter during the first week to get your borrowing privileges activated
- Remote access to electronic resources uses your IUJ portal login

---

## Physical Collection

- Strong holdings in international relations, public policy, business management, and Japanese studies
- English-language collection is extensive; Japanese collection also available
- Borrowing period: ⚠️ confirm current limits at the counter

**Renewing online** (per MLIC library guidance): log into "User Record" (My Library) on the [library website](https://www.iuj.ac.jp/library/) with your IUJ network ID/PW → Status of Borrowing and Reserving → select the item → Renew. Online renewal works **only once, before the actual due date**; on the due date itself the system stops accepting the request. It also does **not** cover course reserve books (one-day loan items) — for those, you have to go to the counter in person.

**Reserving online**: search the catalog on the library website; if a book's status shows ON LOAN, a Reserve button appears. You get a system notice as soon as the book is returned to the circulation desk.

> 💡 Both of these are self-serve online processes as of the most recent MLIC guidance — worth doing before defaulting to a counter trip.

## Extended Loan Periods (Summer Holiday / Graduation)

MLIC runs special due-date arrangements around summer break and graduation — worth knowing the pattern exists, even though exact dates are set fresh each year:
- **Summer holiday special loan period**: general books borrowed shortly before the break get a due date pushed out to the first day of Fall term (One-Year Program students get an earlier due date, tied to their own program's end)
- **Graduating students**: get a special, earlier due date shortly before graduation, so books can be returned before you leave campus

> ⚠️ Framed as illustrative of a recurring pattern (confirmed for 2026), not a fixed annual date — check MLIC's own announcement each year for the actual due dates.

## Interlibrary Loan (ILL)

If IUJ doesn't own a book or article you need:
- Submit an ILL request through the library portal
- Books typically arrive within 1–2 weeks from partner libraries
- Article PDFs often arrive faster (days)
- Free for students, so use it liberally for thesis research

## Reference Librarians

IUJ's library staff are underutilized by most students. They can:
- Help structure a literature search
- Identify databases relevant to your thesis topic
- Advise on citation management tools
- Track down obscure government or policy documents

> 💡 Book a reference consultation early in your thesis process, not after you've spent three weeks spinning your wheels.

## Citation Management Tools

Managing sources manually is a waste of time. Use one of these from Day 1:

| Tool | Best For | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Zotero** | General academic use; integrates with Word/Google Docs | Free |
| **Mendeley** | PDF management + citations | Free |
| **EndNote** | Powerful; preferred by some supervisors | Paid (check if IUJ provides access) |

> 💡 Zotero is the standard recommendation: open source, browser plugin grabs citations automatically, syncs across devices.

## Study Spaces

- Individual carrels and group study rooms available in the library
- Quiet zones vs. collaborative zones: follow the signage
- ⚠️ Booking system for group rooms: confirm at the library counter
- If you're issued a study room locker key, it belongs to the Office of Student Services (OSS), not the library — return it to OSS (and empty/unlock the locker) before you leave campus. Confirmed via a graduating-student reminder; the same logic should apply whenever you stop using the space, not just at graduation.
