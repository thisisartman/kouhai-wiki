Japan is one of the world's best countries to travel within. These are starter itineraries for the most common IUJ student trips — adjust based on your interests and budget.

---

## Tokyo (1–3 Days)

Tokyo is 1.5 hours from Urasa by Shinkansen. Easily done as a day trip or weekend.

### Day Trip (1 Day)
Best for a specific purpose — concert, event, specialist shopping, meeting a contact.
- **Morning:** Arrive Tokyo Station by 10am; head to your main destination
- **Afternoon:** Explore a district (Shibuya, Shinjuku, Asakusa, or Akihabara depending on interest)
- **Evening:** Dinner in Tokyo; catch a late Shinkansen back (~9–10pm last reasonable service)

> 💡 Tokyo day trips are surprisingly doable from IUJ. Use them for concerts, exhibitions, visa-related visits, or just when you need a city fix.

### Weekend (2 Days)
- **Day 1:** Shibuya/Harajuku area, Meiji Shrine, Yoyogi Park; evening in Shinjuku
- **Day 2:** Asakusa (Senso-ji Temple) + Akihabara or Ueno Museum + Yanaka neighbourhood

**Budget:** ~¥25,000–35,000 for transport, accommodation (capsule or budget hotel), and meals.

### 3-Day Tokyo
Add: teamLab digital art museum (book well in advance), Nikko day trip, Kamakura day trip, or neighbourhood deep dives (Shimokitazawa, Nakameguro, Yanaka).

## Kyoto (3–4 Days)

Kyoto is Japan's cultural heart. 3 days is the minimum to do it justice; 4 is better.

**Getting there:** Shinkansen from Tokyo (~2h15m); or from Urasa, change at Tokyo or use the Joetsu to Joetsumyoko and connect. A direct approach is Tokyo → Kyoto on the Tokaido Shinkansen.

**Essential:**
- Fushimi Inari Shrine (伏見稲荷大社) — the famous tunnel of torii gates. Go early morning to avoid crowds.
- Arashiyama bamboo grove — same principle: early morning
- Gion district — historic geisha district; evening walk
- Nishiki Market — Kyoto's street food market; compact and excellent
- Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) — crowded but worth it

**Accommodation:** Book far in advance, especially for spring (cherry blossoms) and autumn (foliage) — Kyoto's peak periods see accommodation prices double.

**Budget:** ¥40,000–60,000 for 3 nights (transport from Tokyo + accommodation + food).

## Osaka (2–3 Days)

Often paired with Kyoto (30 min apart by Shinkansen). The food city.

**Essential:**
- Dotonbori — the iconic canal street with neon signs and street food
- Kuromon Market (黒門市場) — Osaka's kitchen; excellent for seafood and local food
- Osaka Castle — photogenic; good park for a walk
- Namba and Shinsaibashi for shopping and evening entertainment

**Osaka-specific food:** Takoyaki (たこ焼き, octopus balls), okonomiyaki (お好み焼き, Japanese savoury pancake), kushikatsu (串カツ, deep-fried skewers). Do all three.

**Budget:** ¥30,000–45,000 for 2 nights.

---

## Kyoto + Osaka Combined (4–5 Days)

The classic combination. Spend 3 nights in Kyoto, 1–2 in Osaka. Day trip each city from the other.

Add: Nara (奈良) day trip — friendly deer, ancient temples, easily reachable from Kyoto or Osaka.

**Budget:** ¥55,000–80,000. A JR Pass is borderline worth it if you add Hiroshima.

---

## Hiroshima + Miyajima (2 Days)

Often added to a Kyoto/Osaka trip.

- **Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and Museum** — profoundly important; allow 3–4 hours
- **Miyajima Island (宮島)** — floating torii gate, deer, mountain hiking; 30 min by ferry from Hiroshima
- **Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki** — different from Osaka style; try it at Okonomimura (the multi-floor okonomiyaki building)

**Getting there:** From Osaka, ~1.5h by Shinkansen. JR Pass covers this leg if you have one.

## Hokkaido (4–6 Days)

Japan's northernmost main island. Best in summer (lavender fields, cooler temperatures) or winter (powder snow — Niseko is world-class).

**Getting there:** Fly from Tokyo Haneda/Narita to Sapporo (New Chitose Airport) — ~1.5h, ¥8,000–20,000 with LCCs.

**Sapporo base (3–4 days):**
- Sapporo city — Odori Park, Susukino district (nightlife), Sapporo Beer Museum
- Otaru — historic canal town, 30 min by train; excellent seafood
- **Sapporo Ramen** — the city's miso ramen is excellent

**Summer Hokkaido extension:**
- **Furano/Biei** — lavender fields (July); patchwork hills; beautiful cycling country
- **Noboribetsu** — famous onsen resort; volcanic hot spring terrain

**Winter Hokkaido (Ski):**
- **Niseko** — world-famous for powder snow; heavily international; expensive but worth a day if you're skiing
- **Furano ski resort** — less crowded than Niseko; excellent snow

**Budget:** ¥60,000–100,000 for 5 days (flights + accommodation + food + activities).
