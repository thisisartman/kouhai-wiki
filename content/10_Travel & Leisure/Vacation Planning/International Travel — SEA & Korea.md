# ✈️ International Travel — SEA & Korea

Japan's position in East Asia makes it an excellent base for international travel. During winter and spring breaks, many IUJ students make regional trips. This article covers the most common destinations and their visa situation.

---

## General Notes for IUJ Students Travelling Abroad

**Re-entry to Japan:** Make sure your residence status is valid and your re-entry permit is stamped in your passport or handled via the Special Re-entry Permission (特別再入国許可) system.

> ⚠️ If your residence card or visa status has any irregularity, sort it before leaving Japan — re-entry issues are serious. Check with IUJ student affairs if uncertain.

**Passport validity:** Most countries require 6 months of passport validity beyond your intended travel dates. Check this before booking.

**Visa rules change frequently.** Before booking any international trip, verify current entry requirements at the destination country's official immigration website — don't rely solely on this article's visa notes below.

## South Korea (韓国)

**Flight time from Tokyo:** ~2.5 hours
**Budget flights:** Peach, Jeju Air, T'way, Air Seoul from Tokyo Narita/Haneda

### Visa
- **Most nationalities:** Visa-free or e-visa (K-ETA) for short stays
- Requirements vary by passport — some nationalities need to apply for a visa in advance even where most don't. Verify your specific requirement before booking, as policy has changed in recent years.

### Seoul (서울) — 3–5 Days

**Must-do:**
- Gyeongbokgung Palace (경복궁) — the main royal palace; rent a hanbok for photos
- Bukchon Hanok Village — historic neighbourhood
- Myeongdong — shopping and street food (Korean BBQ, hotteok, tteokbokki)
- Hongdae — youthful arts district; good nightlife
- Insadong — antiques, tea houses, craft shops
- DMZ tour — optional but sobering; book through a licensed tour operator

**Food to try:** Korean BBQ (삼겹살, *samgyeopsal*), bibimbap, sundubu jjigae, street food from Gwangjang Market.

**Budget:** ~¥70,000–90,000 for 4 nights including flights from Tokyo.

## Thailand (タイ)

**Flight time from Tokyo:** ~6 hours
**Budget airlines:** Air Asia, Scoot, Thai AirAsia from Narita/Haneda

### Visa
- **Most nationalities:** Visa-free for 30 days (verify current rules — Thailand changed its policy in recent years). Some nationalities get 60 days.

### Bangkok (บางกอก) — 3–5 Days

**Must-do:**
- Grand Palace and Wat Phra Kaew — the gold-roofed royal temple complex
- Wat Pho — massive reclining Buddha
- Chatuchak Weekend Market — one of the world's largest markets
- Khao San Road — backpacker zone; chaotic, worth seeing once
- Day trip to Ayutthaya — ancient capital, ~1.5h by train; absolutely worth it

**Food:** Thai street food is outstanding. Pad thai, som tam, khao man gai, mango sticky rice. Budget ¥1,500–2,000/day for meals.

**Budget:** ~¥60,000–90,000 for 5 nights including flights.

## Bali, Indonesia (バリ島)

**Flight time from Tokyo:** ~7 hours (via Singapore or Kuala Lumpur often)
**Airlines:** Air Asia, Scoot, Singapore Airlines (for direct)

### Visa
- **Most nationalities:** Visa on arrival at Ngurah Rai International Airport (~¥4,000 equivalent, verify current rules before travel)

### Bali — 5–7 Days

**Best areas:**
- **Ubud** — cultural heart; rice terraces, temples, yoga, cooking classes
- **Seminyak/Canggu** — beach, surf, cafés, nightlife
- **Nusa Penida** — island day trip; dramatic cliffs, snorkelling

**Food:** Nasi goreng, babi guling (pork — not suitable for all), satay, fresh fruit. Very cheap — ¥1,000–1,500/day on street food.

**Budget:** ~¥70,000–100,000 for 6 nights including flights. Very affordable on the ground.

## Taiwan (台湾)

**Flight time from Tokyo:** ~3 hours
**Airlines:** Peach, Tigerair, EVA Air, China Airlines

### Visa
- **Most nationalities:** 90-day visa-free
- Some nationalities need to apply in advance (via the Taiwan BOCA website) even where most are visa-free — verify your specific requirement before booking

### Taipei (台北) — 3–4 Days

**Must-do:**
- Taipei 101 — iconic tower; observation deck
- Jiufen — dramatic clifftop old town; the inspiration for Spirited Away's bathhouse aesthetic
- Shilin Night Market — Taiwan's largest night market; essential food experience
- Longshan Temple — active traditional temple in the city

**Food:** Bubble tea (invented here), beef noodle soup, scallion pancakes, stinky tofu, pineapple cake. Night market food is the highlight.

**Budget:** ~¥60,000–80,000 for 4 nights including flights.

---

## Singapore (シンガポール)

**Flight time from Tokyo:** ~7 hours
**Airlines:** Scoot (budget), Singapore Airlines (premium but worth watching for sales)

### Visa
- **Most nationalities:** Visa-free 30–90 days

### Singapore — 3–4 Days

**Must-do:**
- Gardens by the Bay — spectacular; see the Supertrees at night
- Hawker centres — the best cheap food in Southeast Asia (Maxwell, Lau Pa Sat, Old Airport Road)
- Chinatown, Little India, Kampong Glam — distinct neighbourhoods
- Sentosa Island — beaches and cable car

**Note:** Singapore is expensive relative to the rest of SEA — accommodation is ¥8,000–20,000/night depending on type. Budget accordingly.

**Budget:** ~¥80,000–120,000 for 3 nights.
