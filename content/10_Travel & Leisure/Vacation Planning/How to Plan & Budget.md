IUJ's trimester system gives you genuine breaks between terms. Japan is also an excellent base for travel: within the country and across Asia. This article covers the planning and budgeting framework; dedicated articles cover booking and specific itineraries.

---

## When Are the Breaks?

IUJ runs on a trimester system with breaks between terms. Approximate windows:

| Break | Approximate Timing | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Fall → Winter break | Mid-December to early January | ~2-3 weeks |
| Winter → Spring break | Mid-March | ~1 week |
| Spring → Summer (end of year) | Late May / June | Varies by programme |

> ⚠️ Confirm exact academic calendar with IUJ student affairs each year, dates shift.

**Additional travel windows:**
- Long weekends around national holidays
- Short breaks within term (limited, but possible for 2-3 day trips)

## Budget Framework

A rough guide for planning:

### Domestic Japan Travel (per person)

| Trip Type | Budget | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tokyo day trip | ¥15,000-20,000 | Shinkansen + food + activities |
| Tokyo overnight (1 night) | ¥20,000-35,000 | Capsule hotel is ¥3,000-5,000; budget hotel ¥8,000-15,000 |
| Kyoto 3-day trip | ¥35,000-60,000 | Shinkansen + accommodation + food |
| Hokkaido 4-day trip | ¥50,000-80,000 | Flights cheaper than Shinkansen; accommodation varies |
| Ski day trip (Yuzawa) | ¥10,000-15,000 | Lift pass + rental + transport |

### International Travel (from Tokyo/Osaka)

| Destination | Budget (flights + 5-7 days) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Seoul, Korea | ¥50,000-80,000 | Very accessible; budget airlines |
| Bangkok, Thailand | ¥60,000-100,000 | LCC flights from Tokyo/Osaka |
| Singapore | ¥70,000-120,000 | More expensive accommodation |
| Bali, Indonesia | ¥60,000-100,000 | Cheap on the ground |
| Taipei, Taiwan | ¥50,000-80,000 | Short flight; very affordable |

*All estimates assume budget travel (hostels, public transport, street food). Adjust up for more comfort.*

## Planning Priorities

**Book early for:**
- Shinkansen during holidays (Golden Week, Obon, New Year), because seats sell out
- Popular accommodation in Kyoto and Tokyo during peak periods
- Any international flights during school holidays

**Book late (or flexible) for:**
- Last-minute domestic travel outside peak periods (good deals available)
- Budget airline sales (check regularly for Tokyo-Seoul, Tokyo-Bangkok)

## Visa Considerations for International Travel

Japanese residents (on student visa) can travel internationally without losing their status, but:
- Confirm your re-entry permit is valid (多重入国許可, or single re-entry permit)
- If you leave Japan and your residence card or status has issues, re-entry can be refused
- Some destinations require separate visas; check before booking
- Visa-free and visa-on-arrival access varies significantly by passport. See [[International Travel — SEA & Korea]] for country-by-country notes on popular destinations
