# 🎿 Seasonal Activities — Skiing, Hanami, Matsuri

IUJ's location in snow country is a genuine asset for seasonal activities. These are some of the best experiences available during your time here — plan for them.

---

## Winter: Skiing & Snowboarding

Niigata is one of Japan's best ski regions. Multiple world-class resorts are within 20–60 minutes of IUJ.

### Key Resorts

**GALA Yuzawa (ガーラ湯沢)**
- ~20 min from Urasa by Shinkansen (direct from Tokyo too)
- Excellent snow quality, well-maintained runs, beginner to intermediate friendly
- The Shinkansen stops directly at the resort's gondola — no transfers
- English-friendly; popular with international visitors

**Naeba (苗場スキー場)**
- ~40 min by car from IUJ
- One of Japan's largest resorts; long runs, multiple peaks
- Famous for the Fuji Rock Festival site in summer
- Good for intermediate and advanced skiers

**Yuzawa Kogen (湯沢高原)**
- In Yuzawa town (~20 min); multiple smaller resorts
- More local feel; some are cheaper than GALA

**Kagura (かぐらスキー場)**
- Famous for late-season skiing — runs sometimes open until May
- Varied terrain; less crowded than GALA

### Gear

You don't need to buy gear — all resorts have rental shops for boards, skis, boots, and helmets. Daily rental: ~¥3,000–5,000 for a full set.

If you plan to ski frequently, buying second-hand at Hard Off recycle shops saves money. Full ski sets (board/skis + boots) often available for ¥5,000–15,000.

### Cost of a Ski Day

| Item | Approx. Cost |
|---|---|
| Lift ticket (1 day) | ¥5,000–7,000 |
| Gear rental | ¥3,000–5,000 |
| Food on the mountain | ¥1,000–2,000 |
| Transport (if by car, split) | ¥500–1,000/person |

> 💡 Go with a group — carpooling makes transport negligible and the experience is better. Many IUJ students organise ski trips together, especially in January and February.

### GSO Ski Day

The GSO-EC organises an annual **IUJ Ski Day** — a group trip to a local resort (Hakkaisan-Roku in 2026) with organised bus transport and coordinated rental equipment. Registration opens in January and typically fills fast. Payment is in person at CNP; watch for the announcement email from GSO.

### Minamiuonuma City Ski Vouchers

Because IUJ students are registered residents of Minamiuonuma City, you qualify for the city's annual ski lift discount vouchers: **¥2,000 off × 4 coupons per person**, distributed in December.

- **If you live in the dorms (SD1–3):** Coupons are at the **OSS counter** — the city newsletter isn't delivered to dorms, so OSS collects them on your behalf. Pick them up early.
- **If you live in SD4 or off-campus:** Check your mailbox — they arrive in the city magazine in December
- **Valid:** December 1 – March 22 (approximately)
- **Multiple vouchers can be stacked** — two vouchers on a ¥5,000 ticket = ¥4,000 off
- **Eligible resorts (2025–2026):** Ishiuchi Maruyama, Maiko Snow Resort, Joetsu Kokusai, Chateau Shiozawa

> 💡 With four ¥2,000 vouchers, a full day at most of these resorts is essentially free on the lift ticket alone. Don't leave OSS without picking these up in December.

## Spring: Hanami (花見, Cherry Blossom Viewing)

**When:** Late March to mid-April in Minami-Uonuma (later than Tokyo; check local forecasts)

Hanami is the Japanese tradition of gathering under cherry trees as they bloom. It's not hyperbole — it's one of the most beautiful things Japan does, and experiencing it in a smaller town without Tokyo crowds is genuinely better.

**Where to go:**
- IUJ campus — cherry trees on-site
- Riverside parks in Nagaoka — long avenues of sakura along the Shinano River
- Hakusan Park, Niigata City — excellent for a day trip during peak bloom
- Muikamachi area — local spots worth exploring

**What to bring:** Picnic mat, food and drinks, warm layers (Niigata spring can be cold). Japanese tradition is to eat, drink, and appreciate the blossoms — bring the former if you want the latter.

> 💡 Bloom timing varies year to year. Follow the Japan Meteorological Corporation's sakura forecast (桜前線, *sakura zensen*) for current predictions.

## Summer: Festivals & Fireworks

**Nagaoka Fireworks (長岡大花火大会)**
See [[Festivals — Campus & Local]] for full details. Early August; one of Japan's three great fireworks festivals. Do not miss this.

**Local Summer Festivals (夏祭り, *natsumatsuri*)**
Almost every town in Japan holds a summer festival with food stalls, yukata (light cotton kimono), and traditional events. Minami-Uonuma, Nagaoka, and Niigata City all have summer matsuri worth attending.

**Niigata Beaches**
The Sea of Japan coast near Niigata City has beaches accessible in July and August. Not tropical, but a pleasant summer day trip.

## Autumn: Foliage (紅葉, *Kōyō*)

**When:** October–November in Niigata; earlier in the mountains

Autumn foliage (koyo) in the Japanese Alps and surrounding mountains is spectacular. The mountains visible from IUJ turn orange and red before the first snow.

**Good spots:**
- Mountain hiking trails near Minami-Uonuma (Kintoki Yama area)
- Niigata City parks
- Yuzawa area — the combination of mountain colour and onsen is excellent

## Year-Round: Onsen

Rural Niigata has hot spring facilities accessible within 30 minutes. Going to an onsen after a ski day is one of the peak IUJ experiences.
